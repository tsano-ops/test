A Life Remembered
Chapter-by-chapter biography curated by the family
Chapter 1 — 1952–1970

Early Years

Born in a small farming town to Margaret and Thomas Johnson, Robert was the second of four siblings. He grew up surrounded by open fields, long family dinners, and the kind of steady love that shapes a person quietly. His father worked the land; his mother taught piano. Robert took after both — practical hands, gentle voice.

Chapter 2 — 1970–1979

University & Finding His Calling

At nineteen, Robert was the first in his family to attend university. He chose teaching after a single conversation with a high-school history teacher who had refused to give up on him. He earned his Bachelor of Education in 1976 and started his first classroom that same autumn — seventh-grade history, twenty-eight restless students, and a battered set of textbooks. He stayed in that classroom, with that age group, for the next 35 years.

Chapter 3 — 1978

Meeting Mary

They met at a colleague's wedding. Mary later said Robert was the only person in the room who actually listened. He later said she was the only person in the room he wanted to listen to. They married six months after that.

The Day He Stayed Late

It was 1989. I was a brand-new teacher and I'd just had a disaster of a lesson. I stayed after school to cry in the staff room. Robert found me, made me a cup of tea (he kept a kettle in the science prep room — completely against the rules), and told me that the day he stopped being scared of teaching would be the day he should quit. He kept teaching for another 21 years. I've never forgotten that.

PC
Patricia Carmichael
Neighbour for 38 years · 3 weeks ago

The Saturday Tomatoes

Every August he'd appear at my door with an absurd quantity of tomatoes from his garden. He never accepted thanks. Just smiled and said "I grew too many again." He grew too many on purpose, every single year. That was Robert.
