## **2.4. NETWORK CONTACT RECORD**

---

**2.4.1. Introduction**  
**Record Type:** Network Contact Record

**Location:** Me, My Family & My Network → My Network

**Purpose of the Family Member Record**

Purpose of the Network Contact Record  
The Network Contact Record is a dedicated, secure, and beautifully organised space for capturing the people who meaningfully support the PlanOwner’s life — beyond their family circle.  
It brings together identity details, roles, contact information, documents, tasks, shared history, and personal notes, creating a complete portrait of each important relationship.  
Its purpose is to ensure:  
---

✔ **Clarity about who these people are and why they matter**  
Friends, advisors, neighbours, doctors, teachers, colleagues, caregivers — anyone who plays a meaningful part in the PlanOwner’s personal, emotional, or practical life.  
---

✔ **Accurate, always-accessible contact information**  
To support emergencies, medical situations, executor workflows, caregiving coordination, legal actions, or everyday communication.  
---

✔ **Continuity of relationships and responsibilities**  
Executors, contributors, caretakers and other trusted people can understand who this person is and what role they play in the PlanOwner’s life.  
---

✔ **Organisation of tasks, documents, notes, and linked responsibilities**  
Service contracts, medical letters, legal papers, shared tasks, or professional documents related to this contact are stored in one organised place.  
---

✔ **Access transparency during emergencies or loss**  
Executors, guardians, or trusted contacts can quickly understand who this person is and whether they need to be notified, involved, or contacted.  
---

**2.4.2. Creation Methods**  
Network Contacts can be created in multiple contexts to reduce friction and ensure consistent data.  
---

**2.4.2.1. Manual Creation by the User**  
**a. My Network page**  
“+ Add New Contact” opens the Add Contact Form.  
**b. From inside other records**  
**c. From a task, instruction, or workflow**  
Example:  
 Assigning a task → Add Contact  
 Hospital instruction → Add Doctor  
 Pet care plan → Add Sitter  
**d. From document linking \-\>Quick Add**  
When a document relates to someone (e.g., lawyer signature), the system asks:  
 **“Is this person in your network?” → Create new**  
**e. Quick Add**  
A lightweight creation panel appears in:

* Dashboard suggestions  
* Legal flows  
* Health flows  
* Post-Loss Support

  ---

**2.4.2.2. Automatic Creation (System-Generated Records)**  
When the system detects a new person being referenced in content, it may auto-suggest creating a Network Contact.  
Examples:

* User uploads a legal agreement signed by a lawyer  
* User uploads medical letters referencing a specialist  
* User enters a shared memory mentioning a person  
* User assigns a task in the name of someone not in their records  
* A Will, Executor, Witness, or Guardian flow references a new person

  System asks:  
   **“Would you like to add this person to your Network?”**

  If confirmed → a Network Contact placeholder is created.

  ---

**2.4.2.3. Auto-Suggestion & Smart Prompts After Onboarding**  
PlanAfter continues helping users complete their network structure by suggesting useful contacts at meaningful moments.  
Examples:

* “You uploaded a medical document. Add this doctor to your Network?”  
* “You referenced a friend in your Life Story. Add them to My Network?”

  ---

  **Why Multi-Path Creation Matters**

  It ensures:  
   ✔ accuracy of the user’s real-life network  
   ✔ minimal friction  
   ✔ better executor preparedness  
   ✔ better emergency handling  
   ✔ more complete care planning  
   ✔ system-wide consistency

**2.4.3. System Role & Relationship Logic**  
The Network Contact Record uses the same **Person Record architecture** as:

* My Profile  
* Family Members  
* Pets (subset logic)

But with a different **categorisation and relational model**.  
---

**Role in Your Life (Who they are in your life)**  
Examples include:

* Friend  
* Business Partner  
* Doctor  
* Therapist  
* Lawyer  
* Accountant  
* Financial Advisor  
* Insurance Broker  
* Personal Banker  
* Colleague  
* HR Contact  
* Teacher  
* Caregiver  
* Neighbor  
* Other (free text)

This is displayed as the main identity “relationship” on the business card.  
---

**Role in Your Plan  (What role they play in your plan)**  
Network Contacts may hold one or more Plan Roles:

* **Executor**  
* **Contributor**  
* **Beneficiary** (rare, but possible)  
   Life Role and Plan Role are always independent.

---

Relationship Logic  
Network Contacts:

* do **not** appear in the Family Tree  
* do **not** have biological or legal family relationship edges  
* can *convert* into a Family Member Record if needed  
* can be tagged in documents, flows, tasks, memories, and shared access  
* can participate in:  
  * emergency routing  
  * executor workflows  
  * care plans  
  * role-based permissions  
  * sharing and visibility rights

**2.4.4. My Network Record Structure**

1. **Header** 

Header is the “business card” of the record: identity, status, verification, and access.  
The header is identical in logic to the PlanOwner Record, Pets or Network Record. 

2. **Tabs**

The tabs under the Header are:

* Overview  
* Document Entries  
* Memorial \-Memorial Page *(only when deceased)*

---

TAB PURPOSES (Short Summary)

| Tab | Purpose |
| ----- | ----- |
| **Overview** | Displays all structural cards and data sections of the record. |
| **Document Entries** | A centralized view of all Document Entries with the ability to add new ones. |
| **Memorial** *(only when deceased)* | A dedicated space to honor the person’s memory. |

3. **Cards (in Overview tab)**

---

**🟩 1\. Essential Info**  
Subtitle:  
**Identity & key details**

Narrative:  
Keeping this information organised here helps ensure smooth coordination, clearer communication, and fewer uncertainties whenever this person appears in tasks, documents, or future workflows.  
---

🟧 **2\. Contact Info**  
Subtitle:  
**Key contact details**

Narrative:  
Email, phone and other important contact details are stored here for quick and easy communication. These details are used whenever you choose to reach out through PlanAfter—whether to send an invitation, request information, or assign a responsibility. Keeping this information complete ensures your messages are delivered reliably and without confusion.  
---

🟦 **3\. Roles & Access in Your PlanAfter**  
Subtitle:  
**Roles and access permissions**

Narrative:  
This section displays the roles this person holds in your plan and the level of access you choose to grant them. Clear permissions help you stay in control of your privacy and ensure each contact understands how they participate in your plan—especially when responsibilities or tasks are involved.  
---

**🟢 4\. Tasks & Reminders**  
Subtitle:  
**Planning, coordination & follow-ups**

Narrative:  
Tasks and reminders connected to this contact are organised here in one clear view. Whether you need to request a document, schedule an appointment, or follow up on a shared responsibility, everything stays structured and easy to manage. This helps you maintain continuity and coordination without losing track of important actions.  
---

**🟫 5\. Shared With**  
Subtitle:  
**Who can access this record and what they can see**

Narrative:  
This section shows exactly who has access to the information in this record. You stay fully in control of visibility and permissions, ensuring the right people can view or use the information when needed—while your privacy and the contact’s privacy remain protected.
