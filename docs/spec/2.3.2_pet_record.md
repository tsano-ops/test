# **2.3.2. Family Member Record-  PET subcategory RECORD** 

**2.3.1. Introduction**  
**Record Type:** Pet Record

**Location:** Me, My Family and My Network → My Family → Category “Pets”

**Purpose:**

To give the PlanOwner one structured, safe place to store **all important information about their pet**, ensure **continuity of care**, and support **trusted caretakers** if the owner becomes unavailable or passes away \- while also preserving their story, memories, and emotional significance as a true member of the family. **This ensures clarity and continuity of care today, and a meaningful legacy tomorrow.**  
This record ensures continuity of care if the owner is ever unable to, and supports trusted caretakers with everything they need. It is also a space to honor the companionship, history, and meaning this pet adds to the family’s life story.  
***“Your pet is part of your family.***  
***This record keeps everything important about their life, health, identity, documents, and memories in one safe place — so you can take better care of them today, and ensure they’ll be loved and protected if you’re ever unavailable.”***

***Its purpose is to ensure:***  
---

✔ **Your pet’s information is organised in a way that supports real-life caregiving**  
The system understands your pet’s needs, health profile, care routines, and service providers — so it can help with daily care, medical planning, guardianship instructions, and continuity of care.  
---

✔ **Essential documents and details are always accessible**  
Vaccination cards, medical records, microchip documents, insurance papers, and notes are stored neatly and can be found instantly — by you today, and by trusted people in the future.  
---

✔ **Continuity of care across every part of your plan**  
All modules that rely on pet identity and caregiving — Tasks & Reminders, Emergency Contacts, Care Instructions, Post-Loss Support — use this record to stay consistent and reliable.  
---

✔ **A unified place to preserve memories, stories, and emotional legacy**  
Here you can collect photos, stories, achievements, personality notes, reflections, and special moments — creating a beautiful time capsule of the pet’s life and emotional significance.  
---

✔ **Support for the people who may need to care for your pet one day**  
If you are ever temporarily unavailable, incapacitated, or pass away, your executors and trusted caretakers will know exactly where to find all care instructions, medical details, emergency contacts, and routines.  
This reduces stress and uncertainty for your loved ones — and ensures your pet continues to feel safe, protected, and loved.  
---

✔ **A home for both the practical and emotional sides of caring for a pet**  
This record connects medical, emotional, organisational, and day-to-day caregiving information into one complete portrait of your pet.

**2.3.2. Creation Methods**  
A Pet Record can be created in several different ways, ensuring the PlanOwner can easily document and organise their pets.  
---

***2.3.2.1. Manual Creation by the User***  
The user can intentionally create a new Pet Record through:  
**a. Add Family button**  
Located in:

* the Header of My Profile  
* the Header of any Family Member Record  
* My Family page

  This opens the **Add Pet** form where the user fills species, breed, name, age, and initial data.

  ---

  **b. From the Family & Relationships card**

  Inside the PlanOwner Profile Record any Family Record,  the user can add a pet as part of their household.

  Selecting “Pet” opens the Add Pet flow.

  ---

  **c. From the My Family page**

  The “+ Add Pet” button appears within the Pets category.

  ---

  **e. From Link Record flow**

  When linking care routines or medical documents, users can choose:

* Link existing Pet Record  
* Create a new Pet Record

  ---

  ***2.3.2.2. Automatic Creation During Onboarding & Based on User Answers***

  During onboarding, placeholder Pet Records are created when the user indicates they have pets.

  Example:

  If user answers:  
   **“Do you have any pets?” → Yes, 2**

  The system automatically creates:

* Pet 1 – empty record  
* Pet 2 – empty record  
* 

  Each placeholder includes:

* placeholder name (“Pet 1”)  
* species (if provided by user)  
* no personal data yet

  **Purpose of placeholders**

* motivates user to complete records later  
* supports reminders and continuity from day one  
* allows early setup of pet care logic  
* reduces onboarding friction

  ---

  ***2.3.2.3. Auto-Suggestions & System Prompts***

  The platform intelligently proposes creating Pet Records when relevant.

  Examples:

* User uploads a vaccination document → “Create Pet Record for this pet?”  
* User adds a vet appointment → “Which pet is this for? (Create new)”  
* User adds a memory tagged with an animal → “Add to Pet Record?”

  Confirming creates a Pet Record seamlessly.

  ---

  Why a Multi-Path Creation System Matters

  It ensures:  
   ✔ complete and accurate representation of household  
   ✔ minimal onboarding friction  
   ✔ early scaffolding for care routines and reminders  
   ✔ cross-module consistency  
   ✔ user motivation to complete pet profiles


  
**2.3.3. Pets Record Structure**

**Header** 

Header is the “business card” of the record: identity, status, verification, and access.  
The header is identical in logic to the Family Member Record, PlanOwner or Network Record.

**Tabs**

The tabs under the Header are:

Overview  
Document Entries  
Album  
Memorial \- Memorial Page *(only when deceased)*  
---

TAB PURPOSES (Short Summary)

| Tab | Purpose |
| ----- | ----- |
| **Overview** | Displays all structural cards and data sections of the record. |
| **Document Entries** | A centralized view of all Document Entries with the ability to add new ones. |
| **Media Files** | A centralized view of all photos and media, with the option to upload new files. *(You wrote “Album”; if the final tab name is “Album” → replace “Media Files” with “Album”)* |
| **Memorial** *(only when deceased)* | A dedicated space to honor the person’s memory. |

🌟 PETS RECORD — Full Cards 

🟩 1\. Essential Info  
**Subtitle:**  
 Identity & key details  
**Narrative:**  
All key identity details and important documents for your pet can be found here. No more searching through folders, vet emails or paper booklets—everything you need is easy to find when it matters. A complete record helps caregivers, vets and future guardians quickly access the right information to keep your pet safe, healthy and well supported at every stage of life.

---

🟧 2\. Contact Info  
**Subtitle:**  
 Key care & service contacts

**Narrative:**  
Here you keep the most important contact details related to your pet—veterinarians, clinics, groomers, sitters or caregivers. Everything is stored in one place so you always know who to reach out to in emergencies or routine care. Having this information organised ensures smooth coordination for you today, and gives trusted people a clear path to help your pet if you are ever unavailable.  
---

🟦 3\. Medical Info  
**Subtitle:**  
 Health information & vet records

**Narrative:**  
 All medical reports, vaccination history, treatments, allergies and vet documents are stored here—clearly organised and always accessible. This makes vet visits easier, helps you track health changes over time, and ensures caregivers have all the information they need.  
---

🟣 4\. Routines & Care Instructions  
**Subtitle:**  
Personality, habits & comfort

**Narrative:**  
Your pet’s personality, routines, preferences and habits are gathered here—along with any certificates or achievements. These details help anyone caring for your pet understand what brings comfort, joy and safety. Keeping this information organised supports smooth transitions during travel, temporary care, or long-term guardianship, ensuring your pet always feels understood and cared for.  
---

🟢 5\. Tasks & Reminders  
**Subtitle:**  
Care routines, health reminders & renewals

**Narrative:**  
All tasks and reminders related to your pet’s care are listed here — vaccinations, treatments, grooming cycles, food schedules, microchip renewals and more. Everything stays organised in one place so you never miss an important date. These reminders ensure consistent care today and guide trusted caregivers if they ever need to follow your routines.  
---

🟫 6\. Shared With  
**Subtitle:**  
Who can access this record and what they can see

**Narrative:**  
This section shows exactly who has access to your pet’s record and what information they are allowed to view. You stay fully in control of visibility and permissions, ensuring your pet’s sensitive information is protected while still supporting seamless care coordination with the right people.
