## **1.2 Why This Market Matters for PlanAfter**

The online memorial space sits at the intersection of three powerful trends:

* Demographic shift: Aging baby boomer population globally drives demand for end-of-life planning and digital memorialization

* Digital normalization: Post-COVID, virtual funerals and online memorials are now expected, not optional

* Legacy consciousness: 73% of people want future generations to access their life stories digitally

PlanAfter's unique position: While all competitors start AFTER death, PlanAfter starts BEFORE — capturing the person's own data, wishes, and memories while they are alive. This creates a zero-effort memorial for surviving family members, who are otherwise overwhelmed with grief and administrative burden.

# **3\. Industry Trends 2025–2026**

## **3.1 Top 8 Trends Reshaping the Market**

* **Trend 1:** AI-generated obituaries and eulogies

* 2,300 funeral homes now use AI tribute video generators (2023 data)

* 32% improvement in client engagement from AI grief support tools

* Key players: Online-Tribute (AI obituary), Keeper (AI-assisted writing)

* **Trend 2:** QR Code Headstone Technology

* Physical plaques with QR codes linking to digital memorial — fast growing

* Keeper launched KeeperQR; ForeverMissed introduced QR as BETA feature

* Bridges physical gravesite with full digital life story

* **Trend 3:** Hybrid/Virtual Memorial Services

* Now standard for 52% of urban funeral homes globally

* 49% rise in virtual funeral attendance 2021-2023

* In-person \+ online simultaneously is expected, not premium

* **Trend 4:** Subscription vs. Lifetime Pricing Shift

* Industry moving from one-time fees toward subscription models

* Lifetime plans popular with users (lifetime access, no anxiety)

* ForeverMissed: $159.95 lifetime. Farewelling: $169 lifetime

* **Trend 5:** Consolidation & Acquisitions

* Keeper acquired GatheringUs (Dec 2022\) — major consolidation

* Foundation Partners Group acquired Cake (2024)

* Market consolidating around 3-4 major platforms

* **Trend 6:** Mobile-First & No-Account Sharing

* Visitors increasingly don't want to create accounts to contribute

* Online-Tribute: visitors contribute without registration

* WhatsApp sharing now standard for memorial invitations

* **Trend 7:** Donation Integration

* GoFundMe integration built into memorial pages (funeral costs avg $9,000)

* Ever Loved pioneered in-page fundraising — now industry standard

* ForeverMissed added GoFundMe Beta integration in 2025

* **Trend 8:** Personalization & Themed Templates

* 100+ theme libraries now standard (ForeverMissed, Online-Tribute)

* Animated themes with video backgrounds emerging

* Category-specific themes: Children, Veterans, Cancer victims, etc.

* Choose themes that honor the person's personality (without knowing their preferences)

# **9\. Conclusion**

The online memorial platform market is large, growing rapidly, and emotionally powerful — but it has a fundamental architectural flaw: every product waits for death before starting. This creates unnecessary burden for grieving families and results in incomplete, unverified, family-reconstructed memorials.

PlanAfter has the opportunity to redefine this category entirely — not as a competitor to ForeverMissed, but as a platform that makes ForeverMissed-style memorials automatic, effortless, and 10x richer, because the person themselves built it while alive.

The memorial page in PlanAfter is not a feature. It is the proof of the entire platform's value proposition — that planning your life intentionally is the greatest gift you can leave for those you love.
